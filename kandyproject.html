Kandy: Unveiling a Rich Heritage

Embark on a captivating journey to Kandy, Sri Lanka's cultural heartland. This vibrant city boasts a rich tapestry of history and tradition, waiting to be explored. Here are some highlights to entice you:

  • Kandy Lake is also known as Kiri Muhuda or the Sea of Milk. It is an artificial lake in the heart of Kandy, Sri Lanka. Built in 1807 by King Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe next to the Temple of the Tooth. The lake is over 200 years old and covers an area of 6,544 square meters. Visitors can enjoy boat rides and walk around the picturesque lake.
  • Cultural Performances: Immerse yourself in Kandy's vibrant culture through captivating dance performances like Kandyan dance.
  • Heritage refers to things that are passed down from past generations, like traditions, monuments, or values. It is important as it helps us understand where we come from and can provide a sense of identity and belonging. Preserving heritage is crucial to maintain our history and culture for future generations. Heritage sites can include ancient buildings, artifacts, natural landscapes, or even intangible practices like folk music or storytelling traditions..
